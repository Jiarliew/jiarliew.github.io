Product Listing Redesign

The problem

With a wide range of products offered, customers end up confused and unsure about which product could be right for them. With an outdated experience, customers have trouble narrowing down our product offering, ultimately pushing the user back in the journey.

My role

Responsible for the user experience and interaction. Coordinated and worked alongside with product manager, researchers, data analyst, business, engineers, visual design team, and more.

Competitive Analysis

Collected other product listing experiences and documented standard design practices. Patterns we discovered from our data were also points of consideration when looking at other experiences.

Customer input

Ongoing efforts such as surveys and post-purchase questionaires allowed our analyst and research team to gather feedback. Additionally, we conducted interviews and ran workshops with our customers to understand how they interacted with our site.

Consolidate discovery

We ended up with a lot of data coming from different sources. By organizing all of it together, it helped us prioritize certain problems over others.

Step Three: Designing

Beginning the design phase, we sketched out many ideas. Constant review sessions were had to narrow down and focus on a couple ideas.

User testing

Testing was conducted throughout the whole process, even more so during the design phase.

High-fidelity
